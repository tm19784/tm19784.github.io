During the second 'Talking Economics' panel (17/11/21), Graeme Roy of the University of Glasgow claimed 50% of households that are in poverty in Scotland are working households. Despite his main point still holding, the recent figures are actually worse, as illustrated below. 50% was the rough figure at the beginning of the millenium, but this has crept up to a percentage in the mid to high sixties in recent averages. This is likely a result of real wages stagnating across the country.
